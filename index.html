What is Space? üåå

Space is the vast, boundless expanse that lies beyond our planet's atmosphere. It holds everything in the known universe -- planets, stars, galaxies, dark matter, and mysterious phenomena like black holes and quasars.

It is a vacuum, devoid of air, where temperatures can drop to near absolute zero, and distances are measured in light-years.

Planets of the Solar System ü™ê

Our Solar System is made up of eight main planets:

  • Mercury -- the smallest and closest to the Sun.
  • Venus -- has a toxic atmosphere and is hotter than Mercury.
  • Earth -- the only known planet with life! Our home.
  • Mars -- the red planet with frozen water at its poles.
  • Jupiter -- the largest, with a powerful magnetic field.
  • Saturn -- famous for its stunning ring system.
  • Uranus -- rotates on its side and has a pale blue color.
  • Neptune -- the farthest, with intense winds and storms.

Stars ‚≠ê

Stars are massive spheres of hot plasma that shine due to nuclear fusion. Our own star, the Sun, gives us life.

Stars are born in nebulae, live for billions of years, and may die as supernovas, white dwarfs, or black holes.

Galaxies üå†

Galaxies are giant collections of stars, gas, dust, and dark matter. Our galaxy, the Milky Way, has over 100 billion stars!

Types of galaxies:

  • Spiral (like the Milky Way)
  • Elliptical
  • Irregular
"We are made of star stuff." -- Carl Sagan

Black holes are places where gravity is so strong, even light can't escape!

Formed from collapsed stars, their "event horizon" marks the point of no return.
The first-ever image of a black hole was captured in 2019 in the M87 galaxy!

Major Theories About Space üß†

1. Big Bang Theory

The universe began from a singularity 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since.

2. Theory of General Relativity

Einstein showed us that gravity is the bending of space and time by mass. It explains black holes and even time travel-like effects!

3. Time Dilation

Time moves slower near massive objects or at high speeds. Imagine aging slower near a black hole!

4. Multiverse Theory

What if there are other universes besides ours? The multiverse idea is fascinating -- but still theoretical.

5. Inflation theory

The universe went under a rapid expansion faster than light after the big bang.
